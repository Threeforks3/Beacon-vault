# Beacon

Every design decision in Beacon traces to a scenario requirement. This section maps what we built to why we built it.

## Where Beacon Fits

A Coast Guard responder on duty carries four tools to find people: signal mirror, strobe light, whistle, EPIRB. The mirror catches sun. The strobe marks position for hours. The whistle carries sound at close range. The EPIRB brings rescuers near via satellite.

Beacon is the civilian version of that kit. A mirror and SOS strobe in one device — what you'd carry when you're off duty, on the water, and the person who needs to be found. It closes the last mile from the other side of the rescue.

## Signal

- **[[Strobe vs constant-on]]** — Flash draws attention. Constant aids tracking. Beacon does both.
- **[[IR beacon capability]]** — Night vision compatibility for SAR aircraft.
- **[[Visibility range]]** — 2+ nautical miles. Visible from air and surface.
- **[[Color selection]]** — White SOS strobe. Internationally recognized distress signal.

## Power

- **[[Battery life targets]]** — 26+ hours continuous SOS. Spans the critical response window.
- **[[Cold weather performance]]** — Alkaline standard, lithium AAA optional for extreme cold. Same form factor.
- **[[Battery accessibility]]** — 2x AAA — available at any marina, gas station, or supermarket.

## Physical

- **[[Waterproofing]]** — IP68. Survives immersion. Horizontal rain. Salt water.
- **[[Bar-based attachment]]** — Threads onto PFD webbing, pack straps, belt loops. Zero additional parts.
- **[[Impact resistance]]** — Survives drops, impacts, and rough handling.
- **[[Buoyancy]]** — Floats lens-up. Doesn't sink if detached.

## Usability

- **[[One-hand activation]]** — Press and hold. Cold, wet, gloved hands.
- **[[Stuck-button timeout]]** — Prevents accidental drain in a pack or pocket.
- **[[Attachment confidence]]** — One-hand detach with tether backup. Honest about limits.
