# Attachment Confidence

Beacon attaches via bars that slide onto webbing. The design is intentionally quick-detach — one hand, no tools. This is a feature: you want to get it off your pack and into your hand, or move it to a better signaling position, or hand it to someone else.

The security layer is the tether, not the bars. A thin cord loops through the attachment point and clips to your PFD, pack, or belt. If the bars slip, the tether catches. Practical, not avalanche-proof.

## Design Choice: Detachable by Default

Other emergency beacons are permanently mounted or require two-handed release. Beacon is the opposite: pop it off in one motion. This matters when:
- You're in the water and need to hold it above your head
- You're handing your strobe to the helicopter crew chief
- You're moving it from pack to branch for elevated signaling
- You're replacing batteries

The trade-off is that it can detach unintentionally under extreme forces. The tether is there for that case.

## The Tether

Thin spectra or similar cord — 50+ lb break strength, negligible weight. Runs from Beacon's attachment point to a secure anchor (PFD D-ring, pack strap, belt loop). If the bars release, Beacon stays with you. Cost: a few cents, a few grams.

## What This Means For Testing

| Event | Bar behavior | Tether outcome |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Normal carry | Stays put | Not needed |
| One-hand removal | Releases on command | Tether keeps it on you |
| Avalanche tumble | May release | Tether keeps it attached to your body |
| Swift water hydraulic | May release | Tether keeps it attached to PFD |
| Helicopter rotor wash | May release (deliberately) | Tether keeps it — you can hand it to crew chief |
| Drop overboard | Stays on if bars hold, releases if snagged | Tether saves it either way |

The tether has no failure mode where the bars release AND the tether breaks AND you needed the strobe. A force that snaps 50+ lb spectra is a force that has already caused catastrophic injury.

## Open Questions

- Quick-release on the tether? (Deliberate release vs accidental)
- Tether attachment point on the enclosure? (Integrated vs user-tied)
