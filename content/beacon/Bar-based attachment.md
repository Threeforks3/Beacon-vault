# Bar-Based Attachment

Beacon attaches to webbing, straps, packs, and surfaces through two integral bars molded into the cover back face. The bars are closed slots — the strap is always the intermediary. Zero additional parts. Zero assembly. Zero moving components.

## Architecture

The cover back face has two closed bars, each a rectangular through-slot with rounded edges to prevent webbing wear. The bars are features of the cover mold — one mold, no secondary operations.

**Two mounting modes:**

1. **Direct thread:** PFD webbing or pack strap threads through one bar, across the gap, through the other bar. A strap stop sits in the gap between bars — a flat spring clip that grips the webbing and prevents Beacon from sliding. This is the permanent-mount configuration for PFDs, life jackets, and pack straps.

2. **Strap crossover wrap:** A Beacon strap wraps around any surface (PFD chest strap, paddle shaft, pack frame, branch, tent pole) and crosses itself at 90° between the bars. The hook-and-loop closure on the strap secures it. This is the universal-mount configuration for irregular surfaces.

## Forces

The closed-bar design means the webbing must tear or the cover must fracture for Beacon to separate. Tensile testing targets: webbing tear strength at 500+ lbf. Cover bar fracture strength at 300+ lbf. Hydraulic forces in swift water (highest expected force) are below 100 lbf.

**Multi-axis loading:** Avalanche tumble, swift water hydraulic, helicopter rotor wash, water impact — all apply forces in different vectors. The bars are tested against pull (tension along the bar axis), peel (perpendicular to the back face), and shear (sliding along the bar axis). The closed-slot design resists all three.

## Design Rationale

The bar architecture replaced a two-part connector module design. The original concept had a separate connector piece that clipped onto webbing and docked Beacon. This added $1.00+ to the BOM, required a second mold, created a failure point (the clip), and complicated assembly.

The bar collapse: two features on the existing cover mold achieve the same function with zero additional cost, zero additional parts, zero additional assembly steps. The saved mold and assembly cost flows to margin or price reduction.

## Open Standard

The bar dimensions, gap span, and strap interface are published and permissive. Anyone can design accessories that span the same gap: GoPro adapters, tripod mounts, MOLLE clips, suction cups, V-blocks for pole/rail grip. Beacon sells straps at margin. The accessory ecosystem is open — knockoffs are encouraged because they grow the market.
