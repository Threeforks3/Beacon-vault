# Attachment Confidence

Beacon's bar-based attachment must remain connected through the worst forces its users experience. Avalanche tumble. Swift water hydraulic. Helicopter rotor wash. Water impact at speed. The closed-bar architecture is designed so the webbing tears before the bars release.

## Testing Philosophy

**Test to failure.** The question is not "does it hold under normal use?" — it's "what force separates it, and is that force higher than anything a human body can survive?"

**Expected failure modes, in order:**
1. Webbing tears (500+ lbf tensile)
2. Strap closure separates (hook-and-loop shear)
3. Cover bar fractures (300+ lbf estimated)
4. Cover body separates from device (fastener failure)

The victim's body fails before any of these. A force that tears webbing is a force that has already caused catastrophic injury. The attachment is stronger than the person.

## Multi-Axis Loading

| Force Direction | Scenario | Holding Mechanism |
|----------------|----------|-------------------|
| Tension (pull along webbing) | Avalanche tumble, swift water | Closed bars — webbing must tear |
| Peel (perpendicular to back) | Catching on debris, hoist cable snag | Bars are closed — no opening to peel from |
| Shear (slide along bar) | Pack shifting, body position change | Strap stop in gap prevents sliding |

## Open Questions
