# SAR Coalition Feedback Loop

The coalition is not a one-way endorsement. Partner operational experience flows back into Beacon's development. Every firmware change, every attachment option, every signal pattern is informed by the people who use Beacon in real rescues.

## How Feedback Works

1. **Partner deploys Beacon** — in training exercises or real operations
2. **Partner documents** — adds a note to this vault, sends an email, or mentions it in a meeting
3. **Beacon responds** — engineering analysis, firmware change, design revision
4. **Iteration is visible** — the change and its rationale appear in the vault

## Current Feedback Channels

- Direct communication (email, phone, in-person at St. Pete Hub)
- Vault contributions (partners write their own pages)
- Formal evaluation reports (structured after-action reviews)
- SAREX debriefs (informal conversations after training exercises)

## Examples of Anticipated Feedback

| Partner | Scenario | Anticipated Feedback |
|---------|----------|---------------------|
| USCG helicopter crew | Night hoist exercise | "IR strobe acquired at 2.5nm. SOS pattern through NVG is unmistakable. Constant-on mode would help final approach." |
| MRA ground team | Wilderness search | "Strobe visible through canopy at 0.5 mile. Attachment to shoulder strap works. Helmet mount would be better for line-of-sight." |
| Swift water team | River rescue drill | "Device survived repeated submersion. Button press is fine with gloves but 2-second hold is too long in cold water — can't feel the press confirmation." |
