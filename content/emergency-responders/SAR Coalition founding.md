# SAR Coalition Founding

The SAR Guidance Coalition was conceived in 2026 as a channel-to-market strategy for Beacon. Instead of traditional retail launch, Beacon goes to market through the organizations that already do search and rescue — earning their endorsement through transparency, inviting their participation through shared knowledge, and distributing through their communities.

## Genesis

The realization: a life-safety product shouldn't be launched like a consumer gadget. The people who will trust Beacon with their lives — paddlers, hikers, pilots, flood victims — trust SAR organizations more than they trust a startup. If SAR organizations trust Beacon, their communities will too.

**The coalition is the channel.** Partners validate the product. Their endorsement reaches communities that retail advertising can't. Their distribution reaches people who don't shop for emergency equipment until they need it.

## Principles

1. **Humble position.** Beacon's team doesn't claim SAR expertise. We build the signal; SAR professionals teach us how to use it. The knowledge vault is shared — partner contributions are visible alongside Beacon's analysis.

2. **Transparent development.** Every design decision, every test result, every tradeoff is documented in this vault. Partners can see the engineering — challenge it, improve it, or adopt it.

3. **At-cost distribution.** Partners pay manufacturing cost. No margin. This is not a discount program — it's an acknowledgement that partners create the value (trust, community, distribution) and Beacon provides the tool.

4. **Replicable template.** The first coalition (Tampa Bay) is a template. Every region can form its own. The framework, agreements, and vault structure are provided.

## Initial Target

**Tampa Bay / St. Petersburg:**
- USCG Air Station Clearwater (MH-60T Jayhawk, HC-130)
- USCG Auxiliary (co-located at Maritime & Defense Tech Hub)
- Eckerd College SAR program (Ryan Dilkey, FLIR fleet)
- Local MRA-affiliated teams
- Civil Air Patrol Florida Wing
- USCG R&D Center (Fall 2026 engagement)

## Open Questions

- What's the minimum viable coalition for launch credibility? 3 partners? 5?
- Should the coalition have a formal governance structure, or remain an informal advisory network?
- How to handle competing interests? (Two SAR teams serving overlapping regions)
