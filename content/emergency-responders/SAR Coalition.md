# SAR Coalition

The SAR Guidance Coalition is a group of search and rescue organizations, practitioners, and subject matter experts who inform Beacon's design, validate its performance, and distribute it through their communities. Coalition partners receive Beacon at cost with co-branding. They distribute as they see fit — sale or donation. Beacon carries their logo. Their endorsement carries the product.

## Founding Members (Target)

| Perspective | Role | Examples |
|------------|------|----------|
| Maritime SAR | USCG, volunteer marine rescue | Air Station Clearwater, local auxiliary units |
| Mountain/Land SAR | MRA teams, NASAR | Accredited mountain rescue units |
| Air SAR | Civil Air Patrol, helicopter units | CAP wings, Air Force Rescue Coordination Center |
| Swift Water/Flood | FEMA USAR, swift water teams | FEMA task forces, local swift water units |
| SAR Technology | Academic, industry | Eckerd College SAR program (Ryan Dilkey, FLIR fleet) |

## Partner Benefits

- **Beacon at cost** — partners pay manufacturing cost (no margin). Units for evaluation, training, and distribution.
- **Co-branding** — partner logo on the device and strap. Their endorsement is visible to the community.
- **Shared knowledge vault** — this Obsidian vault. Partners contribute scenarios, test results, protocol references. Everyone sees everyone's contribution.
- **Feedback loop** — partner operational experience shapes Beacon's development. Real SAR feedback, not simulated use cases.

## Distribution Model

Partners receive Beacon at cost. They may:
- Sell at whatever price they choose (fundraising)
- Donate to their community members
- Issue to their teams as standard equipment
- Bundle with training programs

Retail sales stack on top of coalition-created demand. The coalition proves the product; retail follows.

## Replication

The coalition model is designed to be replicable. Every city, county, and state can form its own SAR guidance group. The framework, partner agreements, and knowledge vault template are provided. The first coalition (Tampa Bay / St. Pete) serves as the template.
