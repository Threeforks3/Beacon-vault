# USCG Sea School — Rescue and Survival Procedures

**Source:** Boat Crew Handbook — Rescue and Survival Procedures, BCH16114.2A, February 2021. USCG Sea School training materials.

## Strobe Light Specification

From Chapter 3, Section C (Survival Equipment and Pyrotechnics), pages 3-30 to 3-31:

> The strobe light is a lightweight, compact, battery-operated strobe light that emits a high intensity white LED visual distress signal.
>
> The strobe light is used to attract the attention of aircraft, ships, or ground parties. One side is equipped with hook fastener tape so that it can be attached to the boat crew safety helmet, inflatable PFD, or survival vest. This eliminates the need to hold the strobe light, freeing up hands to operate other signaling equipment.
>
> The strobe lights emit approximately 50 to 70 flashes per minute. It will operate for a minimum of 8 hours continuous, but typically 18+ hours. Depending on atmospheric conditions, the strobe light has a visual range of 2 – 5 miles.

**Beacon comparison:** Beacon delivers SOS pattern (internationally recognized distress vs generic strobe), 26+ hour runtime (vs 8h minimum), 850nm IR mode for NVG operations (not available on standard issue), bar-based attachment to any webbing (vs hook-and-loop to specific surfaces).

## Cold Water Survivability

From Chapter 2, Section B (Environmental Factors and Survivability):

> When the human body is submerged in water, heat is lost approximately 26 times faster than it is on dry land.

> Individuals should egress from the water as quickly as possible. A life raft or other floatation device should be used if available. Survival time will increase significantly once out of the water.

### Predicted Survival Time (Table 3-3)

Survival times for an average person in 50°F (10°C) water:

| Condition | Survival Time |
|-----------|--------------|
| Without flotation — floating | 1.5 hours |
| Without flotation — treading water | 2.0 hours |
| With flotation — swimming | 2.0 hours |
| With flotation — holding still | 2.7 hours |
| With flotation — HELP posture | 4.0 hours |
| With flotation — huddle with others | 4.0 hours |

> Contrary to popular belief, the survivor should not swim around in efforts to keep warm. This will only increase the rate of heat loss and speed the onset of hypothermia.

### Core Body Temperature Effects (Table 3-2)

| Core Temp | Symptoms |
|-----------|----------|
| 98.6-96°F (37°C) | Cold sensations, skin vasoconstriction, increased muscle tension |
| 95°F (35°C) | Mental confusion, impairment of rational thought, drowning possible |
| 93°F (34°C) | Loss of memory, speech impaired, sensory/motor function impaired |
| 91°F (33°C) | Hallucinations, delusions, clouding consciousness, shivering stops |
| 88°F (31°C) | Motor performance grossly impaired |
| 86°F (30°C) | Loss of consciousness, no response to pain |
| 80°F (27°C) | Death |

Note: 50% of those in survival situations do not survive at 91°F (33°C) core temperature.

## Darkness and Signaling

From Chapter 2, Section B.4.b (Time of Day):

> In hours of darkness, it becomes critical that the appropriate signaling devices are employed. Mentally, the first few moments an individual is in a survival situation are the most disorientating, however, the survivor must remember that rescue vessels may be nearby during this time. During hours of darkness, flares and lights are crucial when these vessels are near.

## Weather Effects on Visibility

From Chapter 2, Section B.4.a (Effects of Weather on Survival):

> Fog is composed of tiny water droplets hanging in the atmosphere, sufficiently dense to scatter light rays and reduce visibility. Fog makes locating anything more difficult, including survivors in the open water.

> Rain, Sleet, & Snow. As with fog, precipitation can limit the sight of the survivor as well as the rescue party.

## Survival Vest Contents (Table 3-1)

Standard USCG boat crew survival vest carries:
1. Emergency Signaling Mirror (×1)
2. **Strobe Light (×1)**
3. MK-79 Personal Distress Signal Kit (×1)
4. Whistle (×1)
5. MK-124 Marine Smoke and Illumination Signal (×1)
6. Survival Knife (×1)
7. Personal Locator Beacon — PLB (×1)
