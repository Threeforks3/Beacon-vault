# Meet Beacon

Beacon is a methodical answer to a specific problem: in search and rescue, the gap between "someone needs help" and "rescuers can see them" is where people die. Every decision traces to a scenario requirement. Nothing is arbitrary.

## Design Philosophy

Beacon was designed from first principles — not by copying what exists, but by asking what the problem demands. [[Design Philosophy|Read the full philosophy]].

The method: design for the hardest scenarios. If it works when everything is against you — cold water, rough seas, complete darkness — it works across all of them.

## Day Signal

The mirror isn't an afterthought. It's a first-principles answer to the question: what's the most energy-efficient way to be seen in daylight? [[Overt signal mirror|The research behind the mirror bend]].

## Night Signal

SOS — the internationally recognized distress pattern. Not a generic strobe, not a navigation light. [[Strobe vs Constant On|Why SOS, not constant-on]].

LED selection was driven by luminosity requirements, not cost or convenience. [[Color selection|Why these LEDs, at these brightness levels]].

## Beyond Visible

IR exceeds what's required. The 850nm strobe is invisible to the naked eye — preserves night adaptation — but unmistakable through NVG. It's there because SAR aircraft fly with night vision, and a passive target in dark water is nearly impossible to acquire. [[IR beacon capability|Why IR, and why it exceeds specifications]].

## Regulatory

Beacon took the deliberate path. A 1 MHz processor — slow by modern standards — chosen to qualify for FCC §15.103(h) exemption, avoiding $5,000–13,000 in formal testing. Every component decision considers the regulatory path it opens or closes. [[Regulatory Strategy|The regulatory choices and why they matter]].

## Power

Two AAA alkaline batteries. Not lithium, not rechargeable, not proprietary. Available at any marina, gas station, or supermarket. On standby, they last years — ready when they're needed. In operation, 26+ hours of continuous SOS. [[Runtime targets|Battery strategy and runtime]].

## Activation

One button. Press and hold. Deliberate enough to prevent pocket activation, simple enough for cold, wet, gloved hands. No modes, no menus, no screen. [[One Hand Activation|Why one button is the right number]].

## Proven

Tested visibility: 2+ nautical miles from surface and air. [[Visibility range|Measured distances, not estimates]].
