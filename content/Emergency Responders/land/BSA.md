# Boy Scouts of America (Scouting America)

Scouting America (formerly Boy Scouts of America) is the largest youth outdoor education organization in the United States. Through its merit badge program, it formally trains Scouts in search and rescue, emergency preparedness, first aid, lifesaving, and wilderness survival — skills that directly intersect with SAR operations.

BSA is not a SAR organization itself, but its training materials reflect real SAR doctrine. The BSA Search and Rescue merit badge pamphlet teaches the Incident Command System, search tactics, probability of detection, and gear requirements that professional SAR teams use. Many SAR volunteers are former Scouts, and BSA high-adventure bases (Philmont, Northern Tier, Sea Base) maintain their own SAR teams.

## Coalition Position

BSA's value to the SAR guidance coalition is **doctrinal amplification.** BSA's merit badge pamphlets reach hundreds of thousands of Scouts annually. When BSA teaches that a whistle and signal mirror are mandatory SAR gear, that becomes the baseline expectation for a generation of future SAR volunteers and outdoor enthusiasts.

## Key Doctrinal Signals

**"Whistle to signal for help"** — BSA's Family Disaster Kit explicitly designates the whistle as a signaling tool. Both the SAR ready pack and disaster kit mandate whistle + light sources.

**"Three of anything — visual or audible — means distress"** — BSA teaches the international SOS standard across multiple pamphlets. Flashing SOS in Morse code is taught for "night or day."

**"Signaling with a mirror can save your life"** — Mirror signaling technique formally taught in Emergency Preparedness. V-finger sighting method.

**"85 percent of canoeing victims and 48 percent of kayaking victims were not wearing life jackets"** — Direct paddlesport drowning data cited in Lifesaving pamphlet.

**"Active drowning victims cannot reach for aids"** — Validates that signaling must be passive (attached to victim, not requiring reaching/grasping).

## Coalition Notes

- BSA high-adventure bases maintain active SAR teams (PhilSAR at Philmont is the most prominent)
- BSA's Ready Pack gear list aligns with professional SAR team requirements
- BSA teaches ICS — the same command system used by USCG, NIMS, and all federal SAR
- "Cotton kills" doctrine (Search and Rescue pamphlet) is standard wilderness medicine
