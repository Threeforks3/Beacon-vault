# Mountain Rescue Association

The Mountain Rescue Association (MRA) is the national organization of volunteer mountain search and rescue teams in the United States. MRA teams conduct the majority of wilderness and high-angle SAR operations in the U.S. Their operational patterns, training standards, and equipment preferences are directly relevant to Beacon's land-scenario design requirements.

## MRA Structure

- 100+ accredited teams across the U.S. and Canada
- Accreditation requires demonstrated competency in search, technical rescue, and avalanche operations
- Teams are locally organized, volunteer-staffed, and funded through a mix of county support, donations, and grants
- MRA sets training standards and provides inter-team coordination for large-scale searches

## Operational Relevance

**Search patterns:** MRA teams use systematic grid search patterns in wilderness terrain. Ground teams are spaced 10-50 meters apart depending on terrain and visibility. A strobe visible through partial canopy dramatically reduces the search density required — one team can cover a wider swath if the victim is actively signaling.

**Night operations:** MRA teams regularly conduct night searches using headlamps and NVG (on well-funded teams). The IR strobe capability is directly relevant to NVG-equipped MRA teams.

**Equipment culture:** MRA teams are equipment-intensive. Team members carry personal radios, GPS, medical kits, and technical rescue gear. A 70g signaling device on a pack strap is negligible weight for the capability it provides.
