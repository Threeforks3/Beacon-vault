# USCG SAR Doctrine

The United States Coast Guard is the primary maritime search and rescue authority in U.S. waters. Their doctrine, equipment standards, and operational patterns directly inform Beacon's design requirements.

## Key References

- **National Search and Rescue Plan (NSP):** Establishes USCG as the federal maritime SAR coordinator. Defines search phases: Uncertainty, Alert, Distress.
- **Coast Guard Addendum to the National SAR Supplement:** Operational guidance for SAR mission coordinators. Covers search planning, search patterns, and termination criteria.
- **46 CFR Part 161:** Electrical equipment standards. Subpart 161.013 covers electric distress lights for boats — the most directly relevant regulatory framework for Beacon.
- **CG-ENG Policy Letter 03-18:** Self-certification framework for recreational vessel equipment. Establishes the path for non-carriage devices that are not required by regulation but are marketed as safety equipment.

## SAR Phases and Beacon Relevance

| Phase | USCG Action | Beacon Role |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| Uncertainty | Vessel overdue, no distress call | Beacon not yet activated (victim may not know they're being searched for) |
| Alert | Distress call received, position known | Beacon provides positive visual identification at search datum |
| Distress | Active search underway, aircraft/boats on scene | Beacon's strobe/IR provides continuous position marking for search assets |

## Air Station Clearwater

Coast Guard Air Station Clearwater (St. Petersburg/Clearwater area) operates MH-60T Jayhawk helicopters and HC-130 Hercules fixed-wing aircraft. This is the largest air station in the USCG and serves as a primary SAR base for the Gulf of Mexico and Caribbean. Lucky is adjacent to this resource.

**Operational capability:** MH-60T crews fly with NVG (AN/AVS-9) as standard night equipment. HC-130 crews fly high-altitude search patterns with FLIR and radar. Both platforms can detect Beacon's IR strobe at operational ranges.

## USCG R&D Center

The USCG Research and Development Center (New London, CT) evaluates new SAR technologies. Engagement anticipated Fall 2026 when the R&D Center team winters in Florida. The St. Pete Hub provides a natural meeting point.

## Self-Certification Path

Beacon is not a carriage requirement — no vessel is required to carry it. It is marketed as supplemental safety equipment. Under CG-ENG Policy Letter 03-18, manufacturers of non-carriage recreational safety equipment may self-certify compliance with applicable standards. A USCG self-certification bench test plan has been drafted (Beacon ops/ directory).
