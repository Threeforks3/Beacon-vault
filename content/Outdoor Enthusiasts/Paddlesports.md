# Paddlesports — Kayak, Canoe, SUP, Raft

## Float Plan

Paddlers are expected to file a float plan with someone on shore: intended route, put-in time, expected take-out, party size, vessel descriptions, vehicle location. Trip leader or designated shore contact holds the plan. Most paddling fatalities involve paddlers who launched without anyone knowing where they were going.

- USCG Sea School: float plan template with vessel description, itinerary, communication schedule, and abort criteria
- ACA: trip leader is responsible for float plan; solo paddlers should leave a float plan with two independent contacts
- In practice: compliance is low — most recreational day paddlers launch without any plan

## Regs

- **USCG:** PFD required on all vessels (Type I, II, III, or V). Whistle required on all vessels >16ft. Navigation lights required sunset to sunrise. No signal device required on recreational vessels under 16ft unless operating on US waters.
- **State:** Varies — some states require PFD worn at all times for kayak/canoe, some require signal devices on all watercraft
- **ACA:** Recommends PFD, whistle, tow line, spare paddle, pump/bailer, signaling device as minimum kit

## Signals

| Tool | Range | Duration | Limitation |
|------|-------|----------|------------|
| Whistle | ~0.5nm | Unlimited | Audible only — wind/current mask |
| VHF Radio | 5-20nm | Battery-duration | Requires monitoring, channel 16 discipline |
| Cell Phone | Cell-dependent | Battery-hours | Coverage gaps in remote water |
| PLB/EPIRB | Global | 24-48h | 406 MHz alert only — no continuous marking |
| Strobe (Beacon) | 1-5nm | Duty-cycle dependent | Visual line-of-sight only |

Beacon fills the gap between satellite alert and visual acquisition. A paddler in the water drifts away from the GPS position transmitted by a PLB. Beacon marks the actual location with light.

## Environment

- **In water:** Cold-water paddling (below 60°F / 15°C) is common — hypothermia risk increases response urgency
- **On shore:** Isolation, weather exposure, submerged obstacles, current, wave action
- **Night:** Paddler becomes invisible to other vessels without active lighting
- **Dawn/dusk:** Most common time for paddler separation from group

## Community Gear

The recreational paddling community standard kit varies by discipline:

- **Touring kayak:** PFD, paddle float, pump, spray skirt, tow belt, VHF radio, whistle, light, spare paddle
- **Whitewater:** PFD, helmet, throw bag, knife, whistle, tow tether
- **Recreational/SUP:** PFD, leash, whistle, cell phone in dry bag
- **Not commonly carried:** Active strobe, signal mirror, satellite messenger (unless expedition)

**BSA Lifesaving data:** 85% of canoeing victims and 48% of kayaking victims were not wearing PFDs. The population least prepared for an emergency is also the population least likely to carry signaling equipment.
