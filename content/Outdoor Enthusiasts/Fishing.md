# Fishing — Shore, Pier, Kayak, Boat

## Float Plan

Anglers on boats or kayaks should file a float plan. Shore and pier anglers are within sight of others — float plan less critical but still recommended if fishing remote shoreline.

- **USCG:** Vessel operators (including fishing vessels) required to maintain lookout. Float plan recommended for all trips beyond sight of put-in.
- **Kayak fishing:** Growing segment with unique risk — angler is focused on gear/fish, not on conditions. Weight of catch and gear can destabilize kayak.

## Regs

- **USCG:** PFD required on all vessels. Visual distress signals required on coastal waters (night signals for vessels under 16ft). VHF radio recommended.
- **State fishing licenses:** Required in all states. No signal device requirement tied to fishing license.
- **Recreational fishing vessels:** Exempt from some commercial carriage requirements, but PFD and signaling rules apply based on vessel size and operating waters.

## Signals

| Tool | Range | Duration | Limitation |
|------|-------|----------|------------|
| Whistle | ~0.5nm | Unlimited | Audible — ambient noise from water/wind |
| VHF Radio | 5-20nm | Battery | Requires monitoring |
| Cell Phone | Cell-dependent | Battery | Drops signal offshore |
| PLB/EPIRB | Global | 24-48h | One-way — no continuous marking |
| Strobe (Beacon) | 1-5nm | Duty-cycle | Visual — complements satellite alert |

Shore-based angler: unlikely to carry any signaling device beyond cell phone. Boat-based: VHF radio is common, active strobe is not.

## Environment

- **Shore/pier:** Within sight of others, but remote shorelines may be hours from access. Angler on isolated beach with injury has no signaling beyond voice.
- **Kayak fishing:** Weight distribution critical. Cold water immersion risk if capsized. Gear-laden vessel harder to re-enter.
- **Offshore boat:** Distances from shore mean hours before rescue even after alert. Continuous visual marking critical for helicopter/boat acquisition.
- **Night fishing:** Common for certain species. Angler in small boat is nearly invisible to other vessels.

## Community Gear

- **Shore/pier angler:** Rod, tackle, bucket, phone. Signaling gear rare.
- **Kayak angler:** PFD, VHF radio or phone in dry box, anchor trolley, fish finder. Some carry whistle. Active strobe rare.
- **Recreational boat angler:** PFDs, VHF radio, GPS/fish finder. Flare kit if coastal. PLB/EPIRB on larger vessels or extended trips.
- **Gap:** No segment of fishing community carries personal active strobe. Angler separated from vessel or incapacitated on shore has no way to mark position.
