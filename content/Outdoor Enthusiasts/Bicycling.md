# Bicycling — Mountain, Gravel, Road

## Float Plan

Cyclists should leave a route plan with someone who will act if they don't return. For mountain bikers this is critical — they are often deep in trail networks where no one will notice their absence for hours.

- **IMBA:** Ride with a buddy, tell someone your route and expected return, carry ID and emergency contact
- **Mountain bike specific:** Solo riding is common. Rider separated from group after crash can be hours from trailhead. No one knows where they are.
- **Gravel/road:** Route changes mid-ride are common — float plan becomes inaccurate quickly. GPS tracking sharing (Strava Beacon, Garmin LiveTrack) is growing.

## Regs

- No universal signaling requirements for bicycling
- **Night riding:** Front light and rear reflector/light required in most jurisdictions
- **IMBA:** Recommends helmet, repair kit, first aid, communication device, light for all rides

## Signals

| Tool | Range | Duration | Limitation |
|------|-------|----------|------------|
| Whistle | ~0.5nm | Unlimited | Audible — rider may not be able to blow |
| Cell Phone | Cell-dependent | Battery | Many trail systems have no coverage |
| GPS Messenger | Global | Days | Text SOS, subscription |
| PLB | Global | 24-48h | One-way alert |
| Bell/horn | Immediate | Unlimited | Very short range |
| Strobe (Beacon) | 1-5nm | Duty-cycle | Visual — critical for rider who cannot move |

The rider who crashes alone and cannot move has no signaling option beyond voice. An active strobe on pack or helmet closes this gap.

## Environment

- **Remote trail:** Mountain bikers ride in terrain where cell coverage is absent, rescue access is difficult, and passing traffic is rare
- **Injury:** Bicycle crashes produce injuries (clavicle, wrist, concussion) that incapacitate — rider cannot self-extricate
- **Weather:** Heat exhaustion, dehydration, hypothermia. Rider in canyon/bowl with no cell signal and no signaling device depends entirely on chance discovery.
- **Night:** Mountain biking at night is growing. Rider with headlamp and taillight has no distress-specific signal.

## Community Gear

- **Road cyclist:** Helmet, phone, pump, spare tube. GPS computer. ID tag. Signaling gear: none beyond voice.
- **Mountain biker:** Helmet, pack with hydration, repair kit, first aid (basic), phone. Whistle uncommon. Active strobe rare.
- **Enduro/downhill:** Full-face helmet, armor, pack. Rider is far from trailhead and potentially immobile after crash.
- **Bike-packer:** Full overnight kit, satellite communicator more common. Still no dedicated distress signal.

**Gap:** Every cyclist carries a cell phone. Few carry anything that works when the phone has no signal or the rider cannot reach it.
