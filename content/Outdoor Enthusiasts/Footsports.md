# Footsports — Hiking and Trail Running

## Float Plan

The equivalent of a float plan for foot travel: intended trail, departure time, expected return, party composition, vehicle location, emergency contact. Leave it with someone who will act if you don't return.

- **NPS:** "Day hiker off trail" is the most common SAR incident in national parks — 3,400+ SAR incidents annually
- **MRA:** Trip plan with expected return time is the single most effective prevention measure
- **Trail running:** Most runners carry phone only; few leave a plan. Runners far from trailhead on remote trails are invisible until someone misses them

## Regs

- No universal regulatory requirements for hiking or trail running
- **NPS:** Recommends 10 Essentials: map, compass, sun protection, insulation, illumination, first aid, fire, repair kit, nutrition, hydration
- **MRA:** Recommends adding communication device (satellite messenger or PLB) and signaling device for remote/backcountry travel
- **Trail running varies:** Minimalist kit culture conflicts with SAR recommendations

## Signals

| Tool | Range | Duration | Limitation |
|------|-------|----------|------------|
| Whistle | ~0.5nm | Unlimited | Audible — blocked by terrain, wind |
| Cell Phone | Cell-dependent | Battery-hours | No coverage in canyons/backcountry |
| Satellite Messenger | Global | Days | Text-only, subscription |
| PLB | Global | 24-48h | One-way alert |
| Signal Mirror | 10-50nm | Unlimited | Requires sun, skill |
| Strobe (Beacon) | 1-5nm | Duty-cycle dependent | Visual — best at dawn/dusk/night |

**NPS data:** Most common missing equipment in day hiker SAR incidents: light source. A hiker stranded past sunset without illumination cannot signal, navigate, or self-rescue.

## Environment

- **Terrain:** Canyons block cell/satellite. Dense canopy limits visual acquisition from air. Thermal signature through canopy is minimal.
- **Weather:** Rapid changes at altitude. Hypothermia risk even in summer. Whiteout conditions disorient.
- **Night:** Temperature drop accelerates urgency. Hiker without shelter or light faces exposure risk within hours.
- **Trail running specific:** Minimal carried gear by design — runners optimize for weight, not preparedness.

## Community Gear

- **Day hikers:** Water, snacks, phone, maybe a headlamp. Whistle and signal mirror uncommon.
- **Backpackers:** Full 10 Essentials, map, GPS/satellite device, first aid kit, headlamp, whistle.
- **Trail runners:** Phone (often in hand or armband), minimal hydration. No signaling gear beyond phone.
- **Mountain ultrarunners:** Some carry satellite messenger. Almost none carry whistle, mirror, or strobe.

**MRA recommendation:** Active signaling device is a gap in every category of foot traveler. The gear that exists (satellite messengers, whistles) depends on the victim being conscious and capable of activation.
