# Earthquake Entrapment

A major earthquake strikes an urban area. Buildings collapse. Victims are trapped in rubble — in basements, under collapsed floors, in stairwells. They may be conscious, injured, and unable to move. They can hear rescue teams working nearby but can't call out — or rescuers can't hear them over heavy equipment.

## The Scenario

The golden hours: international urban search and rescue (USAR) doctrine identifies the first 72 hours as the critical window for live recovery. After that, survival rates decline sharply. The challenge: finding live victims in thousands of collapsed structures.

USAR teams use acoustic listening devices — they call for silence, then listen for tapping, voices, or movement. But heavy equipment (breakers, saws, generators) masks faint sounds. A victim too weak to shout is effectively silent.

## Current Technologies & Practices

| Technology | Used By | Strengths | Gap |
|------------|---------|-----------|-----|
| Acoustic listening | FEMA USAR, INSARAG¹ | Detects tapping/voices through rubble | Requires silence — heavy equipment pauses; victim must be conscious and mobile |
| Search cameras (optical) | FEMA USAR² | Visual inspection of void spaces | Dust and debris obscure lens; requires line of sight to victim |
| Thermal cameras / FLIR | FEMA USAR³ | Detects body heat through gaps | Concrete and steel block IR; ambient rubble temperature masks signature |
| K9 search | FEMA, international USAR⁴ | Scent detection, independent of victim consciousness | Dogs tire, rotate, limited by shift length. Voids without airflow may not carry scent |
| Cell phone | Victim⁵ | Two-way communication, GPS | Infrastructure destroyed. No power, no signal. Battery dies in hours |

## The Gap

All USAR detection modalities depend on the victim being either conscious and mobile (acoustic) or line-of-sight visible (camera) or thermally distinct (FLIR) or scent-detectable (K9). A victim unconscious in a void space behind concrete — none of these work. An active strobe provides an entirely different detection modality: it doesn't require the victim to move, make noise, or be thermally visible. It just needs to be ON and pointed toward a gap.

## Beacon Design Response

Compact enough for daily carry — earthquakes don't schedule. Under 70g. Dome LED emits collimated beam that cuts through suspended concrete and drywall dust. At close range within a collapsed structure, the strobe is unmistakable even through heavy dust. IR mode provides detection modality for USAR teams with thermal cameras or NVG — some FEMA task forces now carry FLIR-equipped search cameras. Single button, tactile dome — findable by feel in complete darkness. Press and hold 2 seconds. No screen, no sequence. Polycarbonate cover distributes crush forces. Through-hole assembly — no SMD to crack under point loading. CR123A steel jacket inherently crush-resistant. 26+ hour runtime spans the golden 72 hours with battery replacement. Non-sparking — sealed enclosure, no exposed contacts, no ignition risk in gas-leak environments. Battery-powered with no inductive kickback.

## Coalition Footnotes

[¹] **FEMA USAR / INSARAG:** 28 FEMA national task forces. International Search and Rescue Advisory Group standardizes USAR methodology globally. Acoustic listening is primary search technique during breaching operations. Recognized limitation: heavy equipment pauses cost time; faint victims are missed. Active strobe is a detection modality that works DURING breaching — no silence required. [[SAR Coalition partner benefits]]

[²] **FEMA USAR search cameras:** Borescope-style cameras inserted through drilled holes. Optical only — dust and debris obscure the lens. Limited field of view. A strobe visible through the same hole provides positive confirmation before committing breaching resources to that void.

[³] **FEMA USAR thermal imaging:** FLIR-equipped cameras detect temperature anomalies through gaps. Concrete and steel are thermal masses — they mask human body heat. Active IR strobe at 850nm is a point source orders of magnitude brighter than passive body heat — cuts through thermal clutter.

[⁴] **FEMA USAR K9:** Disaster search dogs are the most sensitive detection tool — can detect live scent through meters of rubble. Limitations: shift length (20-minute work cycles), availability, voids without airflow transport. Strobe complements K9 — different detection physics, always on.

[⁵] **Post-earthquake communication:** Cellular infrastructure is destroyed or overloaded. 2023 Turkey-Syria earthquake: survivors with any light source (phone screen, flashlight) were located significantly faster than those relying on sound alone. Limitation: phone batteries died within hours. 26+ hour dedicated strobe extends detectable window. [[Earthquake entrapment#Turkey-Syria 2023]]
